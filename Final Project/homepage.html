El Parque de las Leyendas is a zoo located in the San Miguel district of Lima, the capital of Peru. It was founded by the recognized Lima conservationist Felipe Benavides on March 20th, 1964. Currently, it is managed by the Municipality of Lima and a total area of 970 000 square meters with more than 2070 animals and 215 different species. These specimens are divided among the country's three main ecosystems: the coast, the highlands, and the jungle; in adition to an international wildlife area. Aditionaly, Parqe de las Leyendas has various entertaiment activities for all ages, such as an amusement park, a life-size replica of the Jorge Vargas Fernandez mine, and a reconstruction of the thomb of the Lord of Sipan
